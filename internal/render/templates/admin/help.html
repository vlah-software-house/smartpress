Help & Documentation

Everything you need to know about managing your YaaiCMS site.

Table of Contents

1. Dashboard 2. Posts 3. Pages 4. Content Editor 5. AI Assistant 6. AI Design (Templates) 7. Media Library 8. Categories 9. Users 10. Settings 11. Sidebar & Navigation 12. Security

1 Dashboard

The Dashboard is your landing page after logging in. It provides a quick overview of your site's content.

Statistics cards:

  • Total Posts — number of blog posts (published and drafts)
  • Total Pages — number of static pages
  • Media Files — number of uploaded images and files
  • Users — number of registered user accounts

Quick Actions:

  • New Post — jump straight to creating a new blog post
  • New Page — create a new static page

2 Posts

Posts are time-stamped blog entries displayed in reverse chronological order on your public site.

List view columns:

  • Title — the post headline
  • Status — Published (visible to readers) or Draft (hidden, work-in-progress)
  • Date — when the post was created or last updated
  • Actions — View (open on public site), Edit, or Delete the post

Creating a post:

Click New Post to open the content editor. Fill in the title, write your content using the Markdown editor, set a category, optionally add a featured image, and choose Draft or Published status. See sections 4 and 5 for editor and AI assistant details.

Post-specific fields (not available on Pages):

  • Featured Image — upload an image or generate one with AI (see Media section)
  • Category — assign a single category from the dropdown

Save your post as Draft first to preview it before publishing. You can switch to Published at any time.

3 Pages

Pages are static content (e.g., About, Contact, Privacy Policy) that do not appear in the blog feed. They share the same editor as posts but without Featured Image or Category fields.

How Pages differ from Posts:

  • No featured image field
  • No category assignment
  • Not listed in the blog timeline — accessed by their URL slug
  • Ideal for evergreen content that does not change often

Click New Page to create one. The editing workflow is identical to posts (see section 4).

4 Content Editor

The content editor is used for both Posts and Pages. It is split into a main editing area on the left and an AI assistant panel on the right.

AI Content Generator (top of editor):

When the body field is empty, a prominent AI Content Generator panel appears at the top. Describe the topic you want to write about and click Generate. The AI will write a full article draft in Markdown, pre-filling the editor so you can refine it.

Editor fields:

  • Title — the headline shown to readers
  • Slug — URL-friendly identifier, auto-generated from the title (editable)
  • Featured Image — (posts only) upload from your device, pick from Media Library, or generate with AI. A provider selector lets you choose the image generation service.
  • Category — (posts only) select from existing categories
  • Body — Markdown editor (see toolbar below)
  • Excerpt — short summary displayed in post listings
  • SEO Metadata — meta description and meta keywords for search engines
  • Revision Note — describe what changed in this edit (for your own reference)
  • Status — Draft (hidden) or Published (live on site)

Markdown editor toolbar:

B — Bold I — Italic H1-H3 — Headings > — Blockquote UL / OL — Lists Link — Insert link Image — Insert image (opens Media Picker) Table — Insert table Code — Code block Preview — Toggle rendered preview Side-by-side — Editor + preview split Fullscreen — Distraction-free writing

Use Side-by-side mode to see a live preview while you type. The Image toolbar button opens the Media Picker where you can upload or AI-generate images inline.

5 AI Assistant

The AI Assistant panel sits on the right side of the content editor. It uses the currently active AI provider (configured in Settings) to help you write and optimize content.

Available AI actions:

  • S
    Suggest Titles

    Generates several alternative title suggestions based on your current content. Click any suggestion to apply it.

  • E
    Generate Excerpt

    Creates a concise summary of your post suitable for listings and social media previews.

  • M
    SEO Metadata

    Generates a meta description and keywords optimized for search engines based on your content.

  • R
    Rewrite Content

    Provide instructions in the suggestion field (e.g., “make it more formal” or “simplify the language”) and the AI will rewrite your content accordingly.

  • T
    Extract Tags

    Analyzes your content and suggests relevant tags/keywords that you can use as meta keywords.

Write your content first, then use the AI assistant to polish it. The AI reads your current editor content, so the more you write, the better the suggestions will be.

6 AI Design (Templates)

Templates control the visual appearance of your public website. YaaiCMS uses AI to generate HTML + Tailwind CSS templates that are stored in the database and compiled at render time.

Template types:

  • Header — site-wide navigation bar and branding at the top of every page
  • Footer — site-wide footer with links, copyright, and other information
  • Page — layout for static pages (About, Contact, etc.)
  • Article Loop — layout for blog post listings and individual article views

Template list view:

Shows all templates with their Name, Type, Version number, Status (Active or Inactive), and action buttons. Only one template of each type can be Active at a time. Use the Activate button to make a template the live version.

Generate with AI:

Click Generate with AI to open the AI Template Builder. It features:

  • A template type selector (header, footer, page, article loop)
  • A preview content selector to see the template with sample data
  • A chat-like prompt interface — describe the design you want in plain language
  • A live preview panel showing the rendered result in real time

Restyle All:

The Restyle All tab lets you regenerate all four template types at once with a unified design theme. This ensures visual consistency across your entire site.

Design Themes:

Create and manage reusable design themes to maintain a consistent visual identity. A theme captures the overall style preferences (colors, typography, layout patterns) and can be applied when generating or restyling templates.

Use Restyle All with a design theme for a quick, cohesive site redesign. You can always fine-tune individual templates afterward.

7 Media Library

The Media Library stores all images and files uploaded to your site. Files are stored in an S3-compatible object store.

Features:

  • Grid view — visual thumbnail grid of all uploaded images
  • Upload — click the Upload button to add new files from your device
  • Regenerate All — recreates image variants (thumbnails, optimized sizes) for all media
  • File info — each item shows filename, file type, and size

Supported formats and limits:

  • Formats: PNG, JPG/JPEG, WebP
  • Maximum file size: 50 MB per file

AI image generation:

When setting a featured image on a post, you can generate an image using AI instead of uploading one. A provider selector lets you choose which AI image generation service to use. The generated image is automatically added to your Media Library.

8 Categories

Categories organize your posts into a hierarchical taxonomy. Each post can be assigned to one category.

Managing categories:

  • Add Category — fill in name, slug, description, and optionally select a parent category to create subcategories
  • Edit — modify an existing category's name, slug, or description
  • Delete — remove a category (posts in that category become uncategorized)

Drag-and-drop reordering:

Categories are displayed as a hierarchical tree. Use the drag handle (three horizontal lines) on the left of each category to drag it into a new position. After rearranging, click the Save Order button that appears to persist your changes.

Deleting a category does not delete its posts. They will simply become uncategorized. Remember to click Save Order after rearranging — changes are not saved automatically.

9 Users

Admin only. This section is only visible to users with the Admin role.

Manage the user accounts that can log in to the admin panel.

User list columns:

  • Avatar — user profile picture (initials if none set)
  • Name — display name
  • Email — login email address
  • Role — Admin, Editor, or Author
  • 2FA Status — Active (set up) or Pending setup

Roles:

  • Admin — full access including Users, Settings, and all content management
  • Editor — can manage posts, pages, categories, media, and templates
  • Author — can create and manage their own posts and pages

Actions:

  • New User — create a new user account with name, email, password, and role
  • Reset 2FA — if a user loses access to their authenticator app, an admin can reset their 2FA. The user will be prompted to set up 2FA again on their next login.

10 Settings

Admin only. This section is only visible to users with the Admin role.

General settings:

  • Site Title — displayed in the browser tab and site header
  • Tagline — a short description or slogan for the site
  • Site URL — the public URL of your site (currently read-only)
  • Posts per Page — how many posts appear per page on the public blog listing
  • Timezone — used for displaying dates and scheduling content
  • Language — sets the lang attribute on public pages
  • Date Format — how dates are rendered on the public site (ISO, US, EU, long form, etc.)

AI Provider configuration:

Switch between the four supported AI providers for content generation and template design. Each provider card shows:

  • Provider name — OpenAI, Google Gemini, Anthropic Claude, or Mistral
  • Model — the specific model being used (e.g., gpt-4o, gemini-3.1-pro-preview)
  • API key env var — which environment variable must be set for this provider
  • Status — Active (currently in use), Available (key configured, ready to activate), or No key (environment variable not set)

Click Activate on any Available provider to switch. The change takes effect immediately — no restart required.

AI API keys are loaded from environment variables, not stored in the database. To add a new provider, set the corresponding environment variable (e.g., OPENAI_API_KEY) and restart the application.

11 Sidebar & Navigation

The left sidebar is the primary navigation for the admin panel. It is visible on desktop screens and accessible via the menu button on mobile.

Sidebar sections:

  • Main navigation — Dashboard, Posts, Pages, AI Design, Media, Categories
  • Admin section — Users and Settings (only visible to Admin users)
  • Visit Website — opens your public site in a new browser tab

Collapsing the sidebar:

Click the Collapse button at the bottom of the sidebar to minimize it to icons only. This gives you more horizontal space for the content area. Click the expand button (double chevron) to restore it. Your preference is saved in the browser and persists across sessions.

Mobile navigation:

On smaller screens, the sidebar is hidden by default. Tap the hamburger menu icon in the top-left corner to open it as a slide-over panel. Tap outside the panel or use the close button to dismiss it.

User menu:

In the top-right corner, click your avatar/name to open a dropdown with your display name, email, and a Sign out option.

12 Security

YaaiCMS enforces several security measures to protect your site and user accounts.

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):

  • 2FA is mandatory for all user accounts — it cannot be disabled
  • Uses TOTP (Time-based One-Time Password) compatible with any authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy, 1Password, etc.)
  • On first login, you will be guided through 2FA setup with a QR code to scan
  • After setup, every login requires both your password and a 6-digit code from your authenticator app
  • If you lose access to your authenticator, ask an Admin to reset your 2FA from the Users page

CSRF protection:

All forms include a CSRF (Cross-Site Request Forgery) token. This prevents malicious websites from performing actions on your behalf. Tokens are automatically managed — no action needed from you.

Session management:

Sessions are stored server-side in Valkey (a Redis-compatible store). Your session expires after a period of inactivity, requiring you to log in again. Use the Sign out option in the user menu to end your session explicitly.

Never share your authenticator QR code or recovery codes. If you suspect your account has been compromised, ask an Admin to reset your 2FA immediately and change your password.
